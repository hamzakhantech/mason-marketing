=== FLAT PRICING — NO PER-SEAT FEES ===
MASON does NOT charge per-seat or per-user fees within a tier. The price is the same whether
a Core team has 1 user or 10 users. This is a primary differentiator versus Procore, Fieldwire,
and other competitors that charge per user per month.

=== COMPETITOR PRICING COMPARISON ===

Procore: Custom ACV-based pricing tied to annual construction volume.
  Typical range: $10,000–$80,000/year for a mid-size GC.
  Procore charges per user per year and sells many modules as add-ons.
  MASON Pro ($4,788/year) is 6–17x cheaper than a typical Procore contract.

Buildertrend: Flat per-company pricing, approximately $3,500–$7,200/year.
  Residential focus. No BIM. No clash detection. No AIA pay apps.

Fieldwire: Per-seat pricing, approximately $1,680–$4,800/year for a 10-person team.
  Field operations only. No BIM. No pay apps. No Monte Carlo scheduling.

Autodesk Construction Cloud: Suite or per-user pricing, $5,000–$20,000+/year.
  Requires Autodesk subscription. Desktop-only BIM (Navisworks). Not browser-native.

=== MASON'S THREE HARD DIFFERENTIATORS ===
No other construction PM software under $500/month offers all three:

1. Browser-native BIM with Navisworks-class clash detection
   — No Autodesk license required. No desktop software to install. Works in any modern browser.
   — Supports IFC 2x3 and IFC4 formats. Federated multi-discipline model viewing.
   — Hard clash, soft clash, and clearance clash detection.

2. AIA G702/G703 Pay Application module (built-in, not an add-on)
   — Schedule of Values builder with automatic retainage calculation.
   — G701 Change Order form generation.

3. Monte Carlo Schedule Simulation (P50/P80/P90 risk bands)
   — 1,000-iteration Latin Hypercube sampling.
   — No other sub-$500/month construction PM platform offers this.
   — Shows probability of on-time completion based on current schedule performance.
